Income Tax

What is Income Tax?

Income tax is the main source of income for the government, collected from individuals and businesses based on their income. The government uses tax money to fund roads, healthcare, education and other essential services.

In India income tax is progressive based on slabs, where higher income groups pay higher tax rates. Individuals above a certain limit have to file their Income Tax Returns (ITR) every year to comply with tax laws.

Definition

Income tax is a direct tax collected by the government from individuals, businesses and other entities. It is governed by the Income Tax Act, 1961 and managed by the Income Tax Department of India.

Sources of Taxable Income

When filing income tax, individuals have to disclose income from all sources, including:

  • Salary & Wages – Income from employment.
  • Business & Profession – Profits earned by self employed individuals and businesses.
  • Capital Gains – Profits from sale of shares, properties and other assets.
  • House Property – Rental income from real estate.
  • Other Sources – Interest income, dividends, lottery winnings etc.

Tax Deductions & Savings

Government encourages savings and investments through tax benefits under various sections under Old Tax Regime:

  • Section 80C – Investments in PPF, ELSS, NSC, EPF and life insurance (Deduction up to ₹1,50,000).
  • Section 80D – Health insurance premium deductions.
  • Section 24(b) – Home loan interest deduction.
  • Section 80E – Education loan interest deduction.

Types of Income Tax Returns (ITR)

What is ITR 1 and Its Applicability?

Salaried resident individuals having a maximum income of Rs 50 lakh within a financial year need to choose the ITR 1 form while filing their returns. Here are the criteria that determine their applicability:

  • The income source should be salary or pension
  • Income should be from a single property
  • There can be Income from Other Sources (excluding activities like horse racing, gambling, lotteries, and more).
  • The person should not own any property outside India.
  • He/she should not receive revenue from other countries.
  • Have interest income from SB, Deposits, and other interest income
  • Family pension

What is ITR 2 and Its Applicability?

ITR 2 is applicable for salaried resident individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) and Non-Resident within a financial year. Applicants have to choose ITR 2 in case of the following:

  • Taxpayers not eligible to File ITR-1
  • In case they have income from more than one house property.
  • In case they have income from salary/pension.
  • In case they have Income from Other Sources (including horse racing, card games, lotteries, gambling, etc.).

What is ITR 3 and Its Applicability?

The current ITR-3 Form is to be used by an individual or a Hindu Undivided Family who have income from a proprietary business or is carrying on a profession. The persons having income from the following sources are eligible to file ITR-3:

  • Carrying on a business or profession not opting for presumptive income
  • Carrying on a business or profession who is required to maintain the books of accounts and/or required to get them audited.

What is ITR 4 and Its Applicability?

The current ITR-4 applies to individuals and HUFs, Partnership firms (other than LLPs), which are residents and whose total income includes:

  • Business income according to the presumptive income scheme under section 44AD or 44AE
  • Professional income according to presumptive income scheme under section 44ADA

What is ITR 5 and Its Applicability?

ITR-5 is for firms, LLPs (Limited Liability Partnership), AOPs (Association of Persons), BOIs (Body of Individuals), Artificial Juridical Person (AJP), Estate of deceased, Estate of insolvent, Business trust and investment fund.

What is ITR 6 and Its Applicability?

For Companies other than companies claiming exemption under section 11 (Income from property held for charitable or religious purposes), this return has to be filed electronically only.

What is ITR 7 and Its Applicability?

For persons including companies required to furnish returns under section 139(4A) or section 139(4B) or section 139(4C) or section 139(4D) or section 139(4E) or section 139(4F).

Required Documents for Filing ITR

Essential Documents

When filing your Income Tax Return, you need to keep the following documents handy:

For Salaried Individuals
  • Form 16 from employer
  • Form 26AS (Tax Credit Statement)
  • Bank statements for the financial year
  • Investment proofs for tax deductions
  • Rent receipts (if claiming HRA)
For Business/Self-Employed
  • Books of accounts
  • Bank statements
  • Expense receipts and invoices
  • GST returns (if applicable)
  • Audit report (if turnover exceeds threshold)

Common Documents for All Taxpayers

Identity Documents
  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
Financial Documents
  • Bank Account Details
  • Interest Certificates
  • Dividend Statements
Tax Documents
  • TDS Certificates
  • Tax Payment Challans
  • Previous Year's ITR
Important Note: Always keep your documents organized and readily available before starting the ITR filing process. This will make the process smoother and faster.

Goods and Services Tax (GST)

What is GST?

GST - Goods and Service Tax, is an indirect imposed on the supply of goods and services. It is a multi-stage, destination-oriented tax imposed on every value addition, replacing multiple indirect taxes, including VAT, excise duty, service tax, etc. Goods and services are included under a single domestic indirect taxation law for the whole of India. In this regime, tax is charged at each point of sale.

Types of GST

There are four different types of GST, which are as follows:

State Goods and Service Tax (SGST)

The state government charges SGST on intra-state goods and services transactions. Later, the revenue is collected by the state where the transactions in question were carried out.

Central Goods and Service (CGST)

The central government charges CGST on the intra-state transaction of goods and services. The concerned body is also responsible for collecting the revenue generated through this tax.

GST Registration

As per the GST regime, all business liable to pay service tax, VAT, or central excise have to register under goods and service taxes. An applicant can initiate the GST registration process on the GST portal. Once the application is submitted, the online portal will generate ARN status instantly.

With the help of ARN, an applicant can check his/her application status. Applicants can also post queries if needed. Usually taxpayers will receive their GST registration certificate and GSTIN within a week of their ARN generation.

Important Terms

ARN stands for Application Reference Number and is used to track GST registration application status.

GSTIN is a 15-digit code allotted to every taxpayer registered with GST.

Documents required for GST registration

Sole Proprietor or Individual
  • PAN
  • Address Proof
  • Aadhaar Card (owner)
  • Bank Account details
  • Photograph (Owner)
Partnership Firms inclusive of LLP
  • PAN
  • Address proof (partners and place of business)
  • Bank Account details
  • Copy of Partnership deed
  • Registration certificate or board resolution (for LLP)

GST Returns

There are a total of 13 returns under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) regime. These returns serve to capture different aspects of a taxpayer's financial transactions and obligations within the GST framework.

Return Type Description Who Should File
GSTR-1 Details of outward supplies of goods or services All regular taxpayers
GSTR-3B Monthly summary return All regular taxpayers
GSTR-4 Return for composition taxpayers Composition taxpayers
GSTR-5 Return for non-resident taxable person Non-resident taxpayers

GST Due Dates

Important: Missing GST return filing deadlines can result in penalties and interest charges.

  • GSTR-1 11th of next month
  • GSTR-3B 20th of next month
  • GSTR-7 10th of next month
Quarterly Returns
  • GSTR-1 (Quarterly) 13th of next month
  • GSTR-4 18th of next month

GST Penalties & Interest

Non-compliance with GST regulations can lead to penalties and interest charges. It's important to understand these to avoid unnecessary financial burden.

Note: Late fee should be paid in cash and cannot be paid using Input Tax Credit (ITC).
Late Filing Fees

For Nil Returns: ₹500 (₹250 CGST + ₹250 SGST)

For Other Returns:

  • Turnover up to ₹1.5 crore: ₹2,000 (₹1,000 CGST + ₹1,000 SGST)
  • Turnover between ₹1.5 crore and ₹5 crore: ₹5,000 (₹2,500 CGST + ₹2,500 SGST)
Interest Charges

For Tax Payment Delay: 18% per annum

For Excess Claim of Input Tax Credit: 24% per annum

For Tax Reduction Due to Output Tax Liability: 18% per annum
